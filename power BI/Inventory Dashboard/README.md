Inventory Dashboard Summary
Overview
The Inventory Dashboard provides a comprehensive view of inventory levels, sales performance, and stock movement. Built using power BI and Excel, it helps businesses monitor key inventory metrics, optimize stock management, and make data-driven decisions.

 Tools used
•  Power BI Desktop 
•  Excel 
•  Data modelling: 
              - Product Table
              - Data Tables
              - Status 
              - Inventory Table   

•  Dax Measures including: Total Revenue, Inventory Value, Days of inventory on Hand (doh), Stock Available, Inventory Turnover, Sell-Through Rate, Total Unit Sold

Key Features 
•  KPI panel - with major inventory and sales indicators:
              - Total Revenue
              - Inventory
              - DOH
              - Stock Availability
              - Inventory Turnover

•  Sales Overview Visuals:
             - Monthly sales performance trends
             - Region-based product performance

•  Inventory Breakdown:
             - Inventory by category
             - Product-level metrics including units sold, revenue, stock status, and DOH 

•  Donut chart – highlight inventory turnover for quick visual analysis

•  Interactive filters: 
             - Year/month 
             - Product category

Key insights
•  Total revenue: $495M
•  Inventory Value: $997M, indicating high stock levels
•  Days of Inventory on Hand (DOH): 59 days showing slow inventory movement
•  Stocks Available: 20M units, suggesting overstock issues
•  Inventory Turnover: 12.43, useful for evaluating product speed 
•  Monthly sales trends: peaks and lows guide procurement and promotional planning 
•  Category performers: certain categories significantly outperform others in revenue and unit sold

Business recommendations 
•  Optimize inventory to reduce high stock levels and storage costs
•  Focus marketing or promotion on slower-moving products to improve turnover 
•  Review procurement strategies for categories with high stock and low sales 

Next steps 
•  Add predictive analytics for inventory forecasting 
•  Include supplier lead time analysis for better planning
